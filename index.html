Agroforestry for a climate-resilient Ireland

Emerald Roots

Emerald Roots is a student-led agroforestry initiative supporting farmers, communities, and campuses to plant diverse native trees, restore soils, and build climate resilience across Ireland.

We co-design simple, practical planting plans and connect volunteers with farms that are ready to experiment with climate-smart practices.

Why agroforestry matters

Agroforestry blends trees, crops, and livestock on the same piece of land. Done well, it restores biodiversity, protects waterways, and helps farmers stay productive in a changing climate. Instead of single-species plantations, we focus on mixed native woodlands that work with local soils and communities.

Forest restoration is more than planting rows of trees. Our projects aim to rebuild living ecosystems: layered canopies, healthy soils, and thriving wildlife corridors. We prioritise native species, collaborate with local farmers and community groups, and use each site as a living classroom for students to learn monitoring, mapping, and long-term stewardship.
