Our Mission

In Ireland, agroforestry is increasingly recognised as a vital approach for enhancing soil health, promoting biodiversity, and strengthening rural resilience. Our mission is to advance this sustainable land-use practice by supporting systems that integrate trees with pasture and crops, creating productive landscapes that operate in balance with the natural environment.

Reviving communities through collaborative agroforestry and regenerative practices.

Projects

Our projects are designed to demonstrate the benefits of agroforestry and regenerative land management in Ireland.focus on learning, innovation, and hands-on ecological restoration. Through our Erasmus partnerships, we collaborate with European organisations to exchange knowledge, train young people, and develop forward-thinking approaches to sustainable land use. Alongside this, our Agroforestry Test Sites across Ireland allow us to trial diverse tree–crop–livestock systems, monitor soil and biodiversity improvements, and demonstrate practical solutions for resilient farming. Together, these projects help us build a strong, science-based foundation for regenerative land management and community education.

Erasmus

Our Erasmus projects connect us with partners across Europe to exchange knowledge, develop new skills, and promote sustainable land management. Through training programs, workshops, and cultural collaboration, we empower young people and professionals to explore innovative approaches to agroforestry and environmental stewardship.

Agroforestry Test Sites

Our agroforestry test sites serve as living laboratories where we trial different tree, crop, and livestock combinations. By monitoring soil health, biodiversity, and farm productivity, these sites help us identify practical, scalable solutions for regenerative farming in Ireland. Each test site provides real-world insights that guide our future projects and community outreach.

Agroforestry test sites across Ireland act as living demonstrations of how trees, farming, and biodiversity can work together.

These sites showcase sustainable land use practices that balance productivity with ecological health.
